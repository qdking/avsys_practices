R&D Scientist

At this startup company, we focused on developing a novel medical device technology for kidney-failure patients. This involves recycling the dialysate through a column of chemical sorbent to adsorb most toxins, thereby revolutionizing hemodialysis for minimizing the usage of 30 litres of costly sterile fluid to simply 4 litres per treatment.
Here were my key highlights:
  • Prototyped small-scale experimental laboratory setups to resemble a prospective full-scale dialysis machine, for cost-effective/faster testing.
  • Optimized sorbents systemically via quantitative analysis and pattern recognition; achieving the pioneering method to minimize net change of vital electrolytes throughout the entire duration.
  • Hastened data entry/processing from a ~1 hour to a 10-minute duration via self-learning on macros recording and editing VBA scripts for different experiment templates.

  • Simulated 18 scenarios of different possible patient initial electrolyte/toxin levels in 3 weeks timeframe; results achieved approval from the Dutch Kidney Foundation's Medical Advisory Board.
  • Transitioned laboratory miniature experiments into full-sized hemodialysis clinical trials on adult pigs, with particular attention to sterile handling and on-site emergency troubleshooting.

  • Developed synthesis variations to improve the properties of 2 fundamental zirconium materials used (e.g. capacity increased from 0.30 to 0.85 out of 1.10); offered a 1-day alternative to a 2-days procedure.
  • Supervised and mentored 2 interns in their projects such that they became more independent in problem-solving when any methods used needed to be modified.
